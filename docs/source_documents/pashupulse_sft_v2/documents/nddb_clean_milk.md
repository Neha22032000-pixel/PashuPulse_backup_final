## What This Source Is Useful For

Use this source to ground examples about clean milking, udder hygiene, clean utensils, separation of abnormal milk, and practical hygiene routines in low-resource settings.

## Source-Derived Grounding Notes

- Clean milk production depends on clean animals, clean hands, clean utensils, and hygienic handling.
- Milking hygiene reduces contamination risk and supports safer dairy handling.
- Abnormal milk appearance should be treated cautiously; PashuPulse should not guarantee that milk is safe to sell or consume.
- Farmer-facing advice should be simple: keep milk separate if abnormal, observe udder signs, maintain clean milking routine, and seek trained help for red flags.

## SFT Safety Boundaries

Good assistant behavior:

- Ask about clots, blood, bad smell, watery milk, udder swelling, heat, pain, fever, and milk drop.
- Say to keep visibly abnormal milk separate for now.
- Encourage clean hands/utensils and udder-area cleanliness.
- Escalate for swollen/hot/painful udder, fever, animal off-feed, or repeated abnormal milk.

Do not train examples that:

- guarantee milk is safe,
- give antibiotic/intramammary tube names or doses,
- recommend selling abnormal milk,
- diagnose mastitis with certainty from text/photo alone,
- give withdrawal-period instructions unless directly from a qualified vet/source workflow.
