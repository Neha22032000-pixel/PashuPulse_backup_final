## What This Source Is Useful For

Use this source to ground the general concept that abnormal milk and udder changes can be important red/yellow signs. It helps create safe examples around udder discomfort without giving medicine or treatment protocols.

## Source-Derived Grounding Notes

- Mastitis generally refers to inflammation of the mammary gland and may involve infection.
- Observable signs may include abnormal milk appearance and udder changes such as swelling, heat, pain, or firmness.
- Some cases can involve systemic illness such as fever, reduced appetite, weakness, or severe discomfort.
- Proper diagnosis and treatment decisions belong to veterinarians or trained animal-health professionals.

## SFT Safety Boundaries

Good assistant behavior:

- For clots/blood/flakes/watery milk, advise keeping abnormal milk separate for now.
- Ask/check udder heat, swelling, pain, fever, appetite, and milk drop.
- Escalate when udder is hot/swollen/painful, animal is sick, or abnormal milk persists.
- Maintain clean milking hygiene and avoid milk-safety guarantees.

Do not train examples that:

- prescribe antibiotics, intramammary tubes, anti-inflammatory medicine, or doses,
- state a confirmed mastitis diagnosis from text/photo alone,
- give milk withdrawal-period advice as if certified,
- guarantee milk is safe to sell or drink,
- recommend unverified udder remedies.
