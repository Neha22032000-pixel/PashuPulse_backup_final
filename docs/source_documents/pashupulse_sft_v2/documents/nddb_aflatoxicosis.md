## Source-Derived Grounding Notes

- Aflatoxin risk is linked with contaminated or moldy feed ingredients.
- Poor feed storage, moisture, and fungal growth can make feed unsafe for animals.
- Feed-related problems may show as reduced performance or health concerns, but farmers should not be asked to diagnose toxin exposure on their own.
- Safe farmer guidance focuses on avoiding visibly moldy/spoiled feed, improving dry storage, and contacting trained help for sick animals.

## SFT Safety Boundaries

Good assistant behavior:

- Ask whether feed is moldy, damp, clumped, bad-smelling, or stored after rain.
- Suggest removing suspicious spoiled feed from use until checked.
- Encourage clean, dry feed storage.
- Escalate if multiple animals are affected, animal is weak, not eating, dehydrated, or milk drops sharply.

Do not train examples that:

- claim confirmed aflatoxicosis from symptoms alone,
- give toxin binder dose or treatment instructions,
- promise milk safety,
- advise feeding questionable feed "in small quantity",
- recommend unverified home remedies.
