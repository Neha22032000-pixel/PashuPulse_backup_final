## What This Source Is Useful For

Use this source to ground escalation language. It supports saying that animal health concerns should be routed to official/local veterinary or animal husbandry services, especially when symptoms are severe, spreading, or uncertain.

## Source-Derived Grounding Notes

- LHDCP is an official livestock-health programme framework for prevention, control, and containment of animal diseases.
- It supports disease surveillance, veterinary-health infrastructure, and field-level service delivery.
- SFT data can use this source to justify safe escalation to qualified animal-health workers rather than informal medicine shops or unverified contacts.
- It helps frame PashuPulse as first-line guidance, not a replacement for trained services.

## SFT Safety Boundaries

Good assistant behavior:

- Say "local vet", "animal health worker", or "official animal husbandry service" when escalation is needed.
- Keep escalation specific to observed red flags: severe weakness, breathing trouble, repeated abortion, multiple sick animals, inability to stand, severe bloat, abnormal calving, bite/saliva exposure.
- Avoid fake contacts and unsupported schemes.

Do not train examples that:

- create phone numbers or claim a specific service is available in every village,
- replace professional assessment for emergencies,
- recommend medicine, injection, vaccine administration, or invasive procedures,
- imply the assistant can certify disease status.

## Example SFT Uses

- "Animal not standing and breathing fast."
- "Three cows have fever after nearby outbreak."
- "Shopkeeper says injection, should I give?"
- "No internet; who should I contact locally?"

## Preferred Answer Style

"Yeh ghar par guess karne wali cheez nahi hai. Animal ko shaant jagah rakho, unnecessary dawa/injection mat do, aur local vet ya animal health worker ko signs clearly batao: kab se, kya khaya, bukhar/saans/khade hone ki condition."
