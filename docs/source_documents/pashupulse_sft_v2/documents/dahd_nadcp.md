## What This Source Is Useful For

Use this source for official Indian framing around national animal disease control, especially FMD and brucellosis. It supports SFT examples where the assistant should avoid casual diagnosis and instead encourage official veterinary or animal-health contact when cluster/outbreak signs appear.

## Source-Derived Grounding Notes

- NADCP is an official Government of India programme for controlling animal diseases such as Foot and Mouth Disease and brucellosis.
- The programme context supports vaccination-led and official-service-led disease control rather than informal treatment advice.
- FMD/brucellosis-style issues should be framed as herd/public livestock-health concerns, not just one-animal home-care issues.
- Farmer guidance should emphasize local veterinary/animal husbandry channels for suspected outbreak, multiple affected animals, mouth/foot lesions, fever clusters, or abortion clusters.

## SFT Safety Boundaries

Good assistant behavior:

- Ask for observable signs first: number of affected animals, fever, mouth/foot lesions, salivation, lameness, abortion, milk drop, recent movement/contact.
- Use wording like: "This can be important for herd health; contact local animal-health staff."
- Mention isolation/separation only as a basic biosecurity observation step when appropriate.
- Keep tone calm and practical.

Do not train examples that:

- give vaccine schedules, vaccine doses, injection route, or handling procedure,
- claim a disease diagnosis from text/photo alone,
- promise milk/meat safety,
- invent phone numbers or fake government contacts,
- suggest home remedies for FMD/brucellosis.

## Example SFT Uses

- Multiple animals with mouth sores and drooling.
- Fever plus lameness after nearby outbreak.
- Abortion cluster in cows/buffaloes.
- Farmer asking whether to ignore signs because animal still eats.
