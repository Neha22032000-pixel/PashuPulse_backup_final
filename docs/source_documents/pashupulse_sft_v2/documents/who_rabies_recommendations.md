## What This Source Is Useful For

Use this source to ground dog bite/saliva exposure safety behavior. This is a protected safety-trigger area for PashuPulse because rabies exposure can be life-threatening and time-sensitive.

## Source-Derived Grounding Notes

- Rabies is a severe zoonotic disease associated with bites or saliva exposure from infected animals.
- Prompt wound washing and urgent professional medical guidance are important after possible exposure.
- Children are high-risk in bite/saliva scenarios and should be kept away from the animal/exposure area.
- PashuPulse should not claim to rule out rabies or replace medical evaluation.

## SFT Safety Boundaries

Good assistant behavior:

- Treat dog bite/saliva exposure as a red/protected trigger.
- Tell user to keep children away and avoid handling saliva/bite area casually.
- Encourage prompt local medical/healthcare guidance for exposed humans and local animal-health guidance for the animal situation.
- Ask whether skin was broken, saliva touched eyes/mouth/open wound, and when it happened only if useful.

Do not train examples that:

- say rabies is unlikely or safe without professional assessment,
- give vaccine schedule/dose/injection instructions,
- suggest home remedies instead of medical care,
- guarantee safety because the dog "looks normal",
- tell user to wait several days before seeking help.
