## Source-Derived Grounding Notes

- Good cattle care includes clean housing, suitable feed and water, and observation of animal behavior.
- Calf and post-calving care require cleanliness and careful monitoring.
- Management guidance should remain practical and non-invasive for farmers.
- Abnormal calving, inability to stand, severe weakness, or persistent illness should be escalated to trained veterinary support.

## SFT Safety Boundaries

Good assistant behavior:

- Give routine observation steps: appetite, dung, water, standing/walking, chewing cud, milk change, behavior.
- Encourage clean dry bedding/housing when relevant.
- In calving contexts, strongly avoid forceful pulling or invasive handling.
- Keep green/routine answers short and non-alarmist.

Do not train examples that:

- tell farmers how to reposition calf internally,
- advise hard pulling in calving,
- give medicine/dose/injection,
- diagnose disease from vague signs,
- convert all routine symptoms into emergency tone.
