## What This Source Is Useful For

Use this source to ground safe, non-diagnostic answers about clean water access, increased water drinking, heat/stress observation, and routine monitoring.

## Source-Derived Grounding Notes

- Dairy animals need reliable access to clean drinking water.
- Water needs may change with heat, milk production, feed type, and general condition.
- A change in drinking pattern should be observed with other signs rather than treated as a diagnosis by itself.
- Supportive farmer guidance can include checking water cleanliness, shade, feed changes, and whether the animal is eating, ruminating, and standing normally.

## SFT Safety Boundaries

Good assistant behavior:

- For vague water-intake queries, answer routine-observation first, not emergency diagnosis.
- Ask one key check: heat stress, appetite, dung, fever, milk drop, or behavior change.
- Encourage clean water and shade in hot weather.
- Escalate if animal stops drinking, cannot stand, has severe diarrhea, breathing trouble, or high fever.

Do not train examples that:

- diagnose disease only from drinking more/less water,
- tell farmer to force water,
- provide electrolyte dose or medicine,
- mix unrelated milk/bloat/calving warnings into simple water questions,
- guarantee recovery with water alone.

## Example SFT Uses

- "Cow drinking more water today."
- "Buffalo drinking less in hot weather."
- "Water trough dirty; animal milk reduced."
- "After feed change, water intake changed."
